My friend and fellow designer, Rachel Peeples and I used chance and collaboration throughout this project. We began with a research phase in which we randomly chose five artists/designers that either use chance methodologies or affect change to research. We transitioned from research into play by embracing the methodologies from the artists/designers we learned about. We explored four locations in Detroit and we engaged in exercises to get our ideas flowing like free association word maps and combining newspaper clippings with our photographs. We brainstormed potential forms that the project might take. The ideas were centered around the city, but we felt that we could not represent Detroit so we turned to a handful of its young citizens. We worked with a Detroit charter school and an after-school arts program. We asked students to respond in any medium to a list of questions and we spent time in the classrooms drawing and painting with them. The result of this intense process is a 52" by 68" digital quilt that embodies the intent of a traditional quilt i.e. to tell a story.
